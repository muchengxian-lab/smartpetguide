## 3. 3 Key Findings

### Finding 1: The "no subscription" story is buried
Aorkuler's biggest advantage — zero subscription, ever — is mentioned but not front-and-center in the Amazon listing. Buyers searching "GPS tracker no monthly fee" see the $33 No-Fee tracker first. Aorkuler's $229 premium needs the TCO story: over 3 years, Aorkuler ($229 total) is cheaper than Tractive ($79 + $180-468 in subscriptions = $259-547 total).

### Finding 2: Off-grid use case is under-explained
The RF radio technology is unique but complicated. The Amazon listing says "No phone required" and "3.5 mile range" but doesn't explain *why* this matters. The real story: if your dog chases a deer in a national forest with zero bars, the Aorkuler still works. Every other tracker goes silent. This should be the headline image, not a bullet point.

### Finding 3: 480 reviews is low for the price point
At $229, buyers expect more social proof. Tractive has 8,900 reviews. Aorkuler needs to: (a) actively encourage reviews from verified purchasers, (b) showcase the most compelling use cases (search-and-rescue stories, off-grid adventures), and (c) get covered by outdoor/hiking publications — not just pet sites.

## 4. Content Opportunities

1. **"Aorkuler vs Tractive: 3-Year True Cost Comparison"** — The TCO story is Aorkuler's strongest conversion tool. Create a comparison page or infographic showing total cost over 1/3/5 years.
2. **"What Happens When Your Dog Gets Lost With No Cell Signal?"** — Scenario-based content that dramatizes the off-grid use case. This is the emotional hook that justifies $229.
3. **"Best GPS Trackers for Hiking & Camping With Dogs"** — Aorkuler should own this niche. Partner with outdoor bloggers, not just pet sites.
